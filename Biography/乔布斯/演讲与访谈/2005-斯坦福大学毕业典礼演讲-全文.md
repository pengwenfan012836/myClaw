# 2005 斯坦福大学毕业典礼演讲 —— 全文

> ⭐⭐ "Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish"

---

## 原文

I am honored to be with you today at your commencement from one of the finest universities in the world. Truth be told, I never graduated from college. This is the closest I've ever gotten to a college graduation.

今天我非常荣幸能够与你们一起，出席这个世界上最好的大学的毕业典礼。说实话，我从来没有从大学毕业。这是我离大学毕业最近的一次。

---

I never graduated from college. Truth be told, this is the closest I've ever gotten to a college graduation. Today I want to tell you three stories from my life. That's it. No big deal. Just three stories.

我从来没有从大学毕业。说实话，这是我这辈子最接近大学毕业的一次。今天我想告诉你们我人生中的三个故事。就这样。没什么大不了的。只是三个故事。

---

## Story 1: Connecting the Dots 第一个故事：连接生命中的点滴

I dropped out of Reed College after the first 6 months, but then stayed around as a drop-in for another 18 months or so before I really quit. So why did I drop out?

我在里德学院读了六个月后退学，但之后又以旁听生的身份待了大约18个月，然后才真正退学。所以我为什么要退学？

It started before I was born. My biological mother was a young, unwed graduate student, and she decided to put me up for adoption. She felt very strongly that I should be adopted by college graduates, so everything was all set for me to be adopted at birth, except the fact that when I popped out a couple who insisted that they were going to be the adoptive parents string were exactly the kind of people that would have an adopted child, except they wanted a girl. So my parents, who were on a waiting list, got a call in the middle of the night asking, "We've got an unexpected baby boy; do you want him?" They said, "Of course." My biological mother found out later that my mother had never graduated from college and that my father had never graduated from high school. She refused to sign the final adoption papers. She only relented a few months later when my parents promised that I would someday go to college.

这要从我出生前说起。我的生母是一位年轻的未婚研究生，她决定把我送给别人收养。她非常强烈地认为我应该被大学毕业生收养，所以一切在我出生时都安排好了，除非...一对夫妇在最后一刻改变了主意，他们想要一个女孩。所以当时在候补名单上的我的养父母在半夜接到电话，问："我们有一个意外出生的男婴，你们想要吗？"他们说："当然。"我的生母后来发现我的养母没有大学毕业，我的养父也没有高中毕业。她拒绝签署最终的收养文件。几个月后，当我的父母承诺我总有一天会上大学时，她才勉强同意。

And 17 years later I did go to college. But I naively chose a college that was almost as expensive as Stanford, and all of my working-class parents' savings were being spent on my college tuition. After six months, I couldn't see the value in it. I had no idea what I wanted to study and I didn't see how it would help me figure out what to do with my life. And here I was, spending all of the money my parents had saved their entire life. So I decided to drop out and believed that it would all work out OK.

17年后我真的上了大学。但我天真地选择了一所几乎和斯坦福一样贵的大学，我所有的工薪阶层父母一辈子的积蓄都花在了我的学费上。六个月后，我看不出这有什么价值。我不知道我想学什么，也不知道这会如何帮助我弄清楚我的人生目标。而我却在这里，花着我父母一辈子的积蓄。所以我决定退学，并相信一切都会好起来的。

It was pretty scary at the time, but looking back it was one of the best decisions I ever made. The minute I dropped out I could stop taking the required classes that didn't interest me, and begin dropping in on the ones that looked far more interesting.

在当时这非常可怕，但回想起来，这是我做过的最好的决定之一。我一退学就可以停止上那些我不感兴趣的必修课，开始去旁听那些看起来有趣得多的课。

It wasn't all romantic. I didn't have a dorm room, so I slept on the floor in friends' rooms. I returned Coke bottles to buy food with, and I would walk the seven miles across town every Sunday night to get one good meal a week at the Hare Krishna temple. And I loved it.

但这并不都是浪漫的。我没有宿舍住，就睡在朋友房间的地板上。我收集可乐瓶子去换食物吃，每个星期天晚上步行七英里穿过城市去黑天庙（Hare Krishna temple）吃一顿好饭。我喜欢这样。

And much of what I stumbled into by following my curiosity and intuition turned out to be priceless later on. Let me give you one example.

后来，我出于好奇心和直觉摸索到的许多东西，后来都被证明是无价的。让我给你们举一个例子。

Reed College at that time was perhaps the best calligraphy instruction in the country. Throughout the campus every poster, every label on every drawer, was beautifully hand-calligraphed. Because I had dropped out and didn't have to take the normal classes, I decided to take a calligraphy class to learn how to do this. I learned about serif and sans-serif typefaces, about varying the amount of space between different letter combinations, about what makes great typography great. It was beautiful, historical, artistically subtle in a way that no science course could convey. And I found it fascinating.

里德学院当时可能有全国最好的书法指导。整个校园里，每张海报，每个抽屉上的标签，都是精美的手写书法。因为我退学了，不用上正常的课，我决定上一门书法课来学习怎么做这个。我学习了有衬线字体和无衬线字体，学习了不同字母组合之间的间距变化，学习了是什么让伟大排版变得伟大。它是美丽的、历史的、艺术上微妙的，没有任何理科课程能传达这种感受。我发现它很迷人。

None of this had even a hope of any practical application in my life. But ten years later, when we were designing the first Macintosh computer, it all came back to me. And we designed it all into the Mac. It was the first computer with beautiful typography. If I had never dropped in on that single course in college, the Mac would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts. And since Windows just copied the Mac, it's likely that no personal computer would have them.

这些东西在我生活中完全没有希望有任何实际应用。但十年后，当我们在设计第一台Macintosh电脑时，它们全都回来了。我们把这一切都设计进了Mac。那是第一台有漂亮字体的电脑。如果我从来没有在大学里旁听那一门课，Mac就永远不会有多种字体或比例间距的字体。由于Windows只是抄袭了Mac，可能就不会有任何个人电脑有这些了。

Of course it was impossible to connect the dots looking forward when I was in college. But it was ten years later, again looking backwards, that I felt satisfied.

当然，当我在大学的时候，不可能向前看把这些点点滴滴串联起来。但十年后，当我再回顾过去时，我感到满足。

So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future. You have to trust in something — your gut, destiny, life, karma, whatever. Because believing that the dots will connect down the road will give you the confidence to follow your heart, even when it leads you off the well-worn path, and that will make all the difference.

所以你必须相信这些点会在你的未来以某种方式串联起来。你必须相信某样东西——你的直觉、命运、人生、因果报应，随便什么。因为相信这些点会在未来某处连起来，会给你跟随自己内心的勇气，即使它引导你走上了不那么常规的路，而这会让一切变得不同。

## Story 2: Love and Loss 第二个故事：爱与失落

I was lucky — I found what I loved to do early in life. Wozniak and I started Apple in my parents' garage when I was 20. We worked hard, and in 10 years Apple had grown from just the two of us in a garage into a $2 billion company with over 4000 employees. We had just released our finest creation — the Macintosh — a year earlier, and I was 30. And then I got fired.

我很幸运——我在早年找到了我热爱的事情。沃兹尼亚克和我在20岁时在我父母的车库里创立了苹果。我们努力工作，10年后苹果从车库里的两个人成长为一个拥有4000多名员工的20亿美元公司。我们在一年前刚刚发布了我们最出色的创作——Macintosh，那年我30岁。然后我被解雇了。

How can you get fired from a company you started? Well, as Apple grew we hired someone who I thought was very talented to run the company with me, and for the first year or so things went really well. But then our vision of the future began to diverge. And eventually we had a very fundamental disagreement about where we wanted to go with Apple. And the board sided with him. And so at 30 I was out, and very publicly out. What had been the focus of my entire adult life was gone, and it was devastating.

一个人怎么会被他创立的公司解雇？嗯，随着苹果的发展，我们聘请了我认为非常有才华的人和我一起经营公司，在第一年左右一切都很顺利。但后来我们对未来的看法开始出现分歧。最后我们在苹果的发展方向上产生了根本性的分歧。董事会站到了他那一边。所以30岁时我出局了，而且是公开出局。我整个成年生活的焦点消失了，这是毁灭性的。

I really didn't know what to do for a few months. I felt that I had let the previous generation of entrepreneurs down, that I had dropped the baton as it was being passed to me. I met with David Packard and Bob Noyce, and tried to apologize for getting us into this mess. I was a very public failure, and I even thought about running away from the Valley. But something slowly began to dawn on me — I still loved what I did.

在接下来的几个月里我真的不知道该怎么办。我觉得我让上一代企业家失望了，当接力棒传给我时，我把它掉在了地上。我见到了大卫·帕卡德和鲍勃·诺伊斯，试图为把我们带入这个困境而道歉。我是一个非常公开的失败者，我什至想过逃离硅谷。但渐渐地，我开始意识到——我仍然热爱我所做的事情。

The turn of events at Apple had not changed that one bit. So I decided to start over.

苹果发生的这一切丝毫没有改变这一点。所以我决定重新开始。

I didn't see it then, but it turned out that getting fired from Apple was the best thing that could have ever happened to me. The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner again, less sure about everything. It freed me to enter one of the most creative periods of my life.

我当时没有看到，但事后证明被苹果解雇是发生在我身上最好的事情。成功的重担被重新成为初学者的轻松所取代，对一切都更加不确定。它让我得以进入我人生中最有创造力的时期之一。

During the next five years, I started a company named NeXT, another company named Pixar, fell in love with a wonderful woman who would become my wife, Pixar went on to create the world's first computer-animated feature film, Toy Story, and is now the most successful animation studio in the world.

在接下来的五年里，我创办了一家叫NeXT的公司，另一家叫皮克斯的公司，爱上了将成为我妻子的出色的女人，皮克斯创作了世界上第一部电脑动画电影《玩具总动员》，现在是世界上最成功的动画工作室。

In a remarkable turn of events, Apple bought NeXT, and I returned to Apple, and the technology we developed at NeXT is at the heart of Apple's current renaissance, and Laurene and I have a wonderful family together.

在一个了不起的转折中，苹果收购了NeXT，我回到了苹果，我们在NeXT开发的技术是苹果当前复兴的核心，我和劳伦娜一起有一个美好的家庭。

I'm pretty sure none of this would have happened if I hadn't been fired from Apple. It was terrible tasting medicine, but I guess the patient needed it. Sometimes life's going to hit you in the head with a brick. Don't lose faith. I'm convinced that the only thing that kept me going was that I loved what I did. You've got to find what you love. And that is as true for your work as it is for your lovers.

我很确定，如果我没有被苹果解雇，这一切都不会发生。尝起来很苦的药，但我想病人需要它。有时候生活会用砖头砸你的头。不要失去信心。我确信，唯一让我坚持下去的是我热爱我所做的事情。你必须找到你所爱的东西。对于你的工作如此，对于你的爱人也是如此。

Your work is going to fill a large part of your life, and the only way to be truly satisfied is to do what you believe is great work. And the only way to do great work is to love what you do. If you haven't found it yet, keep looking. Don't settle. As with all matters of the heart, you'll know when you find it. And like any great relationship, it just gets better and better as the years roll on. So keep looking until you find it. Don't settle.

你的工作将占据你生活的很大一部分，唯一真正满足的方法就是做你认为伟大的工作。成就伟大工作的唯一方法就是热爱你所做的事情。如果你还没有找到，继续寻找。不要妥协。和所有心灵的事情一样，当你找到的时候你会知道的。就像任何美好的关系一样，随着岁月的流逝，它会变得越来越好。所以继续寻找，直到你找到它。不要妥协。

## Story 3: Death 第三个故事：关于死亡

When I was 17, I read a quote that went something like: "Live every day as if it were your last, and someday you'll most certainly be right." It left a deep impression on me, and for the past 33 years I have looked in the mirror every morning and asked myself, "If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?" And whenever the answer has been "No" for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something.

当我17岁时，我读到一句话，大意是："把每一天都当作生命的最后一天来度过，终有一天你会发现自己是对的。"它给我留下了深刻的印象，在过去的33年里，我每天早上都会对着镜子问自己："如果今天是我生命的最后一天，我还会想做我今天要做的事情吗？"每当连续很多天的答案都是"不"的时候，我就知道我需要改变一些东西。

Remembering that I'll be dead soon is the most important tool I've ever used to help me make the big choices in life. Because almost everything — all external expectations, all pride, all fear of embarrassment or failure — these things just fall away in the face of death, leaving only what is truly important. Remembering that you are going to die is the best way I know to avoid the trap of thinking you have something to lose. You are already naked. There is no reason not to follow your heart.

记住我即将死去，是我帮助我做人生重大抉择的最重要工具。因为几乎所有的一切——所有外在的期望，所有的骄傲，所有对尴尬或失败的恐惧——这些事情在死亡面前都会消失，只留下真正重要的东西。记住你即将死去，是我知道的避免陷入认为自己会失去什么的陷阱的最好方法。你已经是赤裸的了。没有理由不跟随你的内心。

About a year ago I was diagnosed with cancer. I had a scan at 7:30 in the morning, and it clearly showed a tumor on my pancreas. I didn't even know what a pancreas was. The doctors told me this was almost certainly a type of cancer that is incurable, and that I should expect to live no longer than three to six months. My doctor advised me to go home and get my affairs in order, which is doctors' code for preparing yourself to die.

大约一年前，我被诊断出癌症。我早上7:30做了扫描，它清楚地显示我的胰腺上有一个肿瘤。我甚至不知道胰腺是什么。医生告诉我，这几乎肯定是一种无法治愈的癌症，我应该预计活不过三到六个月。我的医生建议我回家把事情安排好，这是医生们让你准备死亡的暗语。

That meant I was going to have to try to say goodbye to my wife and my children in a few months. It meant trying to come to terms with my own death in a few months. It meant telling my five-year-old son and my daughter what I expected to happen to me over the next few months. It was a chance to leave a gift for those who would carry my name and my legacy forward. But it was not to be. The scan was wrong.

那意味着我必须试着在几个月内和我的妻子和孩子们说再见。它意味着试图在几个月内接受我自己的死亡。它意味着要告诉我五岁的儿子和女儿，在接下来的几个月里，我期望发生什么。这是一次机会，可以为那些将继承我的名字和遗产的人留下一个礼物。但这不会发生。扫描结果是错误的。

The reason they couldn't get a biopsy was because of the risk of spreading the cancer. And so I lived with that diagnosis. But about a month later, my doctors told me they had made a mistake. They had biopsied the wrong organ. And that was the best news I ever heard.

他们无法进行活检的原因是癌细胞可能会扩散。所以我带着那个诊断结果生活着。但大约一个月后，我的医生告诉我他们搞错了。他们在错误的器官上做了活检。这是我听过的最好的消息。

During that time I have come face to face with death, and I have a much clearer view of what is important in life. I can see that my initial response to this experience was probably the best thing I could have done. Death is very likely the single best invention of life. It is life's change agent. It clears out the old to make way for the new. Right now the new is you, but someday not too long from now, you will gradually become the old, and be cleared away.

在那段时间里，我直面死亡，对什么是生命中真正重要的东西有了更清晰的认识。我可以看到我对这次经历最初的反应可能是我能做的最好的事情。死亡很可能是生命唯一的最佳发明。它是生命的变革者。它清除旧的，为新的让路。现在你是新的，但不久之后的某一天，你会逐渐变老，然后被清除。

Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life. Don't be trapped by dogma — which is living with the results of other people's thinking. Don't let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice. And most important, have the courage to follow your heart and intuition. They somehow already know what you truly want to become. Everything else is secondary.

你的时间是有限的，所以不要浪费它去过别人的生活。不要被教条所困——那是活在别人思考的结果里。不要让别人的意见淹没了你内心的声音。最重要的是，要有勇气追随你的内心和直觉。它们在某种程度上已经知道你真正想成为什么。其他一切都是次要的。

When I was young, there was an amazing publication called The Whole Earth Catalog, one of the bibles of my generation. It was created by a fellow named Stewart Brand out in Stanford, and he brought it to life with his poetic touch. This was in the late 1960's, before personal computers and desktop publishing, so it was all made with typewriters, scissors, and Polaroid cameras. It was sort of like Google in paperback form, 35 years before Google came along. It was idealistic, and it was full of great tools and great notions.

当我年轻的时候，有一本很棒的出版物叫做《全球概览》，它是我们那一代人的圣经之一。它是由一个叫斯图尔特·布兰德的人在斯坦福创办的，他用诗意的方式让它充满活力。那是在20世纪60年代末，在个人电脑和桌面出版出现之前，所以它是用打字机、剪刀和宝丽来相机制作的。它有点像纸质版的Google，比Google早了35年。它是理想主义的，充满了很棒的工具和很棒的想法。

Stewart and his team put out several issues of The Whole Earth Catalog, and then when they had run out of things to say, they published a final issue. And it was as sophisticated as all the other issues. On the back cover of their final issue was a photograph of an early morning country road, the kind you might find yourself hitchhiking on if you were so inspired. And it said, "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish." And it was their farewell message as they signed off. Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish.

斯图尔特和他的团队出版了几期《全球概览》，然后当他们已经没有什么要说的了，他们出版了最后一期。它和前面几期一样精致。在最后一期的封底，是一张清晨乡村道路的照片，如果你有灵感的话，你可能会发现自己正搭便车走在那条路上。上面写着，"保持饥饿。保持愚蠢。"这是他们告别时发出的信息。保持饥饿。保持愚蠢。

And I have always wished that for myself. And now, at graduation, I wish that for all of you.

我一直希望自己能做到这一点。现在，在毕业典礼上，我祝愿你们所有人也能做到。

Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish.

**保持饥饿。保持愚蠢。**

Thank you all, very much.

非常感谢大家。
